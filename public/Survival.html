Survival Guide: From Day 1 to Diamond Tools

Starting a new world in Survival can feel overwhelming, but the first few minutes matter the most. You want to get the basics done quickly: gather wood, craft a few tools, and find somewhere safe before night hits. Once you’ve made it through the first night, the game opens up and you can start exploring, mining, and building properly.
The first thing you should do is collect wood. Break a few trees, turn the logs into planks, and make a crafting table. With that, you can craft sticks and your first set of wooden tools — nothing fancy, but they get the job done for now.
After that, upgrade to stone as fast as you can. Mine a bit of exposed stone or dig down a little until you find it. Stone tools last longer and work faster, which makes everything else smoother.
Before the sun goes down, make a small shelter. It can be anything — a dirt box, a little hole in a hill, or a quick starter base. The point is just to avoid mobs at night so you don’t get overwhelmed on your first day.
You’ll also want a furnace early on so you can cook food and smelt materials later. Coal helps with torches, but if you can’t find any, smelting wood into charcoal works just as well. Lighting your area is important because mobs spawn in dark spaces.
Getting a bed should be one of your goals on day one. If you find sheep nearby, grab three wool and craft one. Setting your spawn point saves you from losing your progress if something goes wrong.
For long-term survival, you’ll want a steady food source. You can farm wheat, carrots, and potatoes, or hunt animals until you get a proper farm going. Exploring caves is also a big part of progression, but make sure you bring torches and avoid digging straight down.
As you gather more materials, you’ll start organizing your base, expanding your farms, and crafting better armor and tools. A shield helps a lot in combat, especially against skeletons. Hitting only when the attack meter fills gives you more damage and keeps battles from dragging on.
